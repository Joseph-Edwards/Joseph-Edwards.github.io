I wouldn't consider myself a conspiracy theorist, but I'm certain that there has to be some
ulterior, nefarious reason for why spinach comes in such large bags. I bought a bag last week, I
feel like I've eaten some every day, and yet there is still about two thirds of a bag left in the
fridge, crying out to be eaten. Not to be defeated by Big Spinach's agenda, today's meal attempts to
use up my remaining leafy greens, and also a courgette I had knocking about. Enter the room:
**sausage and spinach orzo**.

My ingredients were inspired by Mob's recipe for [Creamy Garlicky Prawn
Orzo](https://www.mob.co.uk/recipes/creamy-garlicky-prawn-orzo) (substituting prawn for sausage and
cream for Greek yoghurt), but the method strays quite a bit. In Mob's recipe, you are instructed to
prep all of your ingredients and then encase them in a baking-paper envelope before cooking in the
oven. The liquid from the grated courgette should steam the orzo, and you're left with a delicious
dish. That felt like too much faff for a weeknight meal, so I opted to whack everything in one pan
instead.

I started off by browning my sausage in a pan, followed by a quick deglaze with some white wine
vinegar. Next, it was in with garlic, shredded courgette and lemon zest. Once those flavours had
mingled, I added the orzo and yoghurt. It was all looking a bit dry, so I added a splash of water to
the mix. Just before the end, I added the spinach to wilt, a squeeze of lemon juice, and
reintroduced the sausages.

## Rating

This dish was really enjoyable; I liked that the acidity from the vinegar and the lemon offset
the creaminess, and sausage always makes me happy. I also like the fact that I was able to use up
quite a few bits and bobs from my fridge. Unfortunately, my keenness to eat got the better of me, so
my orzo was slightly underdone. Additionally, as ever, my presentation leaves a lot to be desired.

Overall, a tasty dish with room for improvement.

7/10
